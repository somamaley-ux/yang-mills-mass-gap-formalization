# Yang--Mills Extension Paper Status

Last updated: 2026-04-19

## Main result

The clearest current win in this standalone export is the extension-paper side.

The local-net base is now treated as fixed, and the extension stack makes the
analytical mechanization of four critical seams explicit in Lean rather than
leaving them only as broad route claims:

- the QE3 density / graph-core handoff
- the dyadic-to-continuous-time OS upgrade
- the continuum transport / sharp-gap bridge
- the Section 8 endpoint / admissibility bridge

These were the main load-bearing transitions that most needed source-grounded
reconstruction. In the current export they are no longer vague bridge slogans.
They are isolated as named theorem surfaces, reduced payloads, proof-home
projections, source-route recoveries, and reconstruction equalities.

## Manuscript posture

The extension stack now tracks the nonlocal endpoint manuscript in a recognizably
paper-shaped way.

- Section 4 is represented by a frozen theorem-scope extended-support class
  with explicit support, label, and deformation language.
- Section 7 is represented by the same-local-shadow / different-global-form
  distinction problem and its sector-level recovery surface.
- Section 8 is represented by a scope-faithful completion and admissibility
  bridge over the unchanged local vacuum theory.

So the export now says more than "some sector language exists." It says what
the paper's theorem scope is, what the local net forgets, what the sector layer
recovers, and how the endpoint bridge is meant to sit over the fixed local
theorem object.

## What Is Now Strong

- The four critical seams are explicitly named and isolated.
- The QE3 side is reconstruction-explicit from smaller public and source-route
  payloads.
- The OS upgrade seam is reduced to a sharp smaller payload and tracked back to
  its main source routes.
- The continuum transport seam is rebuilt from lower transport/gap ingredients.
- The Section 8 endpoint bridge is no longer only a downstream slogan; it is
  reconstructed from endpoint proof-home and Route 1 endpoint-package
  ingredients through the preferred theorem layer.
- The canonical paper-facing theorem-scope package now reaches the completion
  bridge, Clay-endpoint package, and packaged Section 7/8 corollary surfaces.
- The Section 4 theorem-scope layer now also has explicit canonical package and
  bridge names in code, not only “preferred paper-facing” aliases.
- The compatibility layer now explicitly names a canonical manuscript-facing
  Section 8 theorem surface and a canonical combined Section 7 plus Section 8
  corollary.
- The public docs now foreground this extension-paper interpretation rather
  than burying it inside broader archive summaries.

## Residuals

The remaining abstraction is narrower than before.

- The most paper-faithful concrete instantiation of the theorem-scope
  extended-support class is now canonically named in code, but still wants its
  most paper-faithful final manuscript presentation.
- The final Section 8 residual is now more specific: the canonical packaged
  endpoint surfaces exist, and the canonical theorem surface is named, but the
  compatibility layer still foregrounds a few equivalent patched theorem /
  bundle / bridge formulations that need to be further demoted behind that
  standard route. Several of the most visible structured, boundary-bridge, and
  patched-assumption-bundle routes have already been explicitly collapsed back
  onto the canonical theorem.

So the honest current reading is:

- the four critical seams are mechanized strongly enough to be the main visible
  result,
- the remaining gap is mainly theorem-scope presentation refinement and final
  theorem-packaging cleanup,
- and the export still does not claim a full first-principles Lean derivation
  of all cited Yang--Mills analytic proof homes.

For practical project management, this extension stack can now be treated as
closed for the moment. It should be reopened only for genuine manuscript-
faithfulness refinement, referee-facing precision work, or new mathematical
content.
