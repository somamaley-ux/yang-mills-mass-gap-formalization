Current honesty boundary
------------------------

What is now encoded honestly:

- a first-pass fixed-base theorem surface for rigidity
- exhaustion by five mechanisms
- family-level classification statements for the five flagship case-study families
- appendix-level completion and forcing readback decompositions
- disposition/closure packaging
- the main conservative-vs-external dichotomy
- the no-internal-reentry corollary shape

What is not yet encoded as full local mathematical machinery:

- detailed partial-algebra semantics for old tuples and primitive graphs
- detailed local semantics for saturation, universal constructions,
  canonical representative rules, and full readback-map algebra
- the detailed family-level semantics behind saturation,
  universal constructions, and representation detours

Bottom line
-----------

The current Lean support provides an honest fixed-base theorem surface for the
paper's main claims, including the appendix-level completion/forcing readback
split.

It should still be described as a lightweight-to-intermediate fixed-base
formalization, not as a full mechanization of every family-level and
case-study-level detail in the manuscript.
