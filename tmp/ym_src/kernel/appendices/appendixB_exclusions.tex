\section{What the kernel intentionally excludes, and why}\label{app:kernel-exclusions}

This appendix records the material intentionally left out of the proof kernel. The exclusion rule is simple: if a file or theorem block does not carry a live theorem-level edge on the packet DAG of Section~\ref{sec:kernel-spine}, it does not belong in the kernel.

In short: the kernel is minimal in proof burden, not necessarily in page count. It includes every live theorem packet and the critical seam details, and it excludes everything that would force experts to spend time separating live mathematics from packaging.
