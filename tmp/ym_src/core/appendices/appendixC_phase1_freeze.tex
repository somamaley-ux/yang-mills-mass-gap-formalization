\section{Phase-I proof-surface freeze: master theorem index and canonical numbering crosswalk}\label{app:phase1-freeze}

This appendix is the Phase-I freeze document for the live proof surface. It is purely navigational and adds no mathematics. The authoritative live theorem surface of the hardened suite consists only of the four compiled roots --- \texttt{core.tex}, \texttt{companion1.tex}, \texttt{companion2.tex}, and \texttt{companion3.tex} --- together with the theorem-facing appendices compiled inside those roots. The tables below freeze one canonical label, one source-manuscript crosswalk, one packet assignment, one proof home, one theorem role, and one immediate live-consumer list for every load-bearing or endpoint-facing node on the public spine.

\subsection*{Live proof surface versus reserve materials}

\begin{quote}\small
Support PDFs, archival source facsimiles not compiled in the four live roots, source-only routing appendices such as Appendix~P and Appendix~Q, build notes, and editorial memoranda are reserve materials only. They add no mathematics, carry no unique proof step, and are not part of the proof burden. If any theorem-local implication appears only in reserve material, that implication must be moved back into one of the four live roots before the suite is considered synchronized.
\end{quote}

\paragraph{Canonical numbering rule.}
The core-paper labels are the public canonical labels. Companion/source labels are retained in the proof homes and are frozen here only as crosswalk labels. The ``Immediate live consumers'' column records theorem nodes and endpoint clauses on the live spine, not every narrative mention.
