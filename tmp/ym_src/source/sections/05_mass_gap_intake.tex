\section{Lane B: downstream OS intake and spectral-gap reformulation}
\label{sec:mass_gap_intake}

Throughout Lane~B we work in the OS vacuum representation of the local gauge-invariant net generated by
$\mathcal A_{\mathrm{loc}}$.
Accordingly, the spectral gap obtained below is the \emph{vacuum gap} for this local net and makes no claim about
possible additional representations/superselection sectors arising from nonlocal line or surface operators;
see \Cref{cor:vacuum_gap_scope,cor:global_form_main_scope}.
The primary proof of \Cref{thm:main}(D) is the Route~1 chain in Appendix~F:
\[
\text{one-shot entrance}\ \Longrightarrow\ \text{full fixed-lattice OS gap}\ \Longrightarrow\ \text{continuum sharp-local vacuum gap}.
\]
The Wilson-versus-patched seam is already closed at fixed scale by Theorem~\ref{thm:Wilson_patch_transport}.
Appendix~F now packages the remaining fixed-lattice-to-continuum step into the theorem-level pair Theorem~\ref{thm:phys_gap_noncollapse} and Corollary~\ref{cor:route1_QE3_gap}.
At the mixed-channel seam, the Lane~A input is now routed through the literal telescoping-summand reduction of Lemma~\ref{lem:finite_cap_mixed_channel_reduction} together with the single-slot bounded-factor estimate of Proposition~\ref{prop:finite_cap_bounded_factor_insertion}, rather than through a fixed-word proxy.
Lane~B is included only as a downstream spectral-calculus reformulation of that same sharp-local gap statement, using the dense/graph-core package already isolated in Lane~A and the Euclidean estimate already derived in Appendix~F for the same state.
