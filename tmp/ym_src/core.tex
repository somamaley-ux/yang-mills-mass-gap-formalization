\begin{abstract}
This paper proves a theorem about the bounded-region local gauge-invariant sharp-local net generated by flowed curvature composites for every compact connected simple gauge group $G$. The mass gap is proved for the vacuum Hamiltonian in the OS/Wightman reconstruction of that local net. Faithful-Wilson universality is theorem-level qualitative only: within the admissible gradient-flow renormalization class and after coherent normalization, different faithful finite-dimensional unitary Wilson regularizations yield canonically isomorphic continuum local theories. The explicit Packet~1 / Route~1 quantitative ledger retained here remains indexed by the chosen faithful regularization $\rho$. Extended-support line and surface sectors, and more generally nonlocal sector data, lie outside the theorem-level claim. The core paper organizes the proof through a finite public packet reduction with one ultraviolet/public-scope gate, one live Route~1 lattice-to-continuum vacuum-gap chain, one live Lane~A sharp-local construction chain, and one claim-facing endpoint packet; the detailed analytic proofs remain in Companions~I--III.
\end{abstract}
