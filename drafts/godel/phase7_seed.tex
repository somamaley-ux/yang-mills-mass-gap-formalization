\begin{abstract}
This paper does not contest Godel's incompleteness theorems in their standard metamathematical setting. It proves instead a narrower application theorem: no Godel-style \emph{same-domain} threat architecture is instantiable inside a fixed governance-sensitive regime already forced into a kernel of inferential governance, standing-bearing reuse, lawful tensor-preserving redescription, unique admissible interior, coherent legal trajectory structure, and irrecoverable failure. Relative to that kernel, all downstream interface, quotient, and coherent-reasoning layers are treated as forced consequences rather than as fresh body burdens. The coherent-reasoning layer closes the hardest hostile attack by forcing coherence to be pointwise standing-preservation along legal same-scope trajectories, by prohibiting deferred coherence, by making terminal standing history-sensitive certification, and by collapsing every coherence-relevant distinction into tensor content rather than skin. Once inferential life is shown to be exhausted by standing classification and coherent legal trajectory structure, governance-relevant code predicates collapse, same-domain diagonalization fails, semantic / infinitary / meta-foundational / richer-same-domain rescue routes fail or become explicit scope change, and no same-domain incompleteness-objection route remains. The target is not bare derivation-trace classification, and provisional mathematical practice is classified separately from standing-bearing same-domain acts.
\end{abstract}
