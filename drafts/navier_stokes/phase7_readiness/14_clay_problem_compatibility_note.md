# Clay problem / rule compatibility note

This note is non-proof-bearing. It records the relation between the manuscript's theorem target and the Clay problem statement / prize rules.

## Theorem target
The manuscript is written to solve the **periodic** Navier-Stokes problem in the form stated by Fefferman for the Clay problem set: smooth divergence-free periodic initial data on the three-dimensional torus, with a global smooth solution / pressure pair in the same classical periodic scope.

## Publication-rule posture
The Clay prize rules are not proof ingredients. They govern what would have to happen *after* a correct proof exists:
- publication in a qualifying refereed mathematics venue,
- public mathematical examination,
- at least two years of general acceptance before prize consideration.

## Practical consequence for this project
- The manuscript PDF must stand on its own mathematically.
- The readiness pack exists to support refereeing and public scrutiny, not to supply hidden proof content.
- No claim of prize eligibility should appear in the theorem body.
