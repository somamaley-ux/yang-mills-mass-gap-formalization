# Post-publication scrutiny plan

## Objective
Prepare the manuscript for the multi-year public examination contemplated by the Clay rules.

## Immediately after journal submission
- Freeze the submitted source bundle and public hash.
- Keep a theorem-local objection log using the response template.
- Do not post side memoranda that carry live proof burden.

## If the paper is sent to referees
- Respond referee-by-referee with label-local replies.
- Fold any proof-bearing correction into the manuscript.
- Refresh the source hash and QA report after each revision.

## After acceptance / public appearance
- Maintain a public errata page and version archive.
- Encourage seminar treatment by analysts, foundations readers, and generalists.
- Welcome written critiques that identify named theorem labels or primitive assumptions.
- Track whether any objection is genuinely new or already settled by the current theorem map.

## First two years after publication
- Keep the theorem-local response log current.
- Preserve every public version of the manuscript and hash manifest.
- Encourage third-party expository notes that summarize the proof spine without adding proof burden.
- If a substantive correction occurs, publish it openly and promptly.

## Clay-facing posture
The goal is not to “avoid criticism.” The goal is to make criticism theorem-local, public, and auditable, so that acceptance or rejection happens on mathematical grounds visible from the published paper.
