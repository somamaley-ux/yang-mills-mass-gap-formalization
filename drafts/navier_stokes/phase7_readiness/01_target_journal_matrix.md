## Governing rule
Under the official Clay rules, a prize-eligible solution must appear in a refereed mathematics publication of worldwide repute (or other form later deemed qualifying by the SAB) and then achieve general acceptance for at least two years. Direct submission to CMI is not the route.  
Official rules page: https://www.claymath.org/millennium-problems/  
Official PDF rules document: https://www.claymath.org/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/millennium_prize_rules_0.pdf

## Operational decision
1. Submit first to **ARMA** with a concise cover note emphasizing:
   - exact match to Fefferman periodic statement (B),
   - standalone PDF with no proof-bearing supplement,
   - theorem-local closure,
   - manuscript-local quotient / factorization / no-dynamics packets,
   - no appeal to off-paper corpus.
2. If rejected without full review, revise any expository issues raised by the editor and move to **APDE**.
3. Treat **JEMS** as a prestige backup only if the author is comfortable with the current length issue and the likely longer editorial cycle.

## Submission posture
- One journal at a time.
- No simultaneous submission.
- No private side memorandum should be offered as proof support.
- If an editor asks for clarifying expository material, it should either be folded into the manuscript or explicitly marked as non-proof-bearing.
